> "The only way to do great work is to love what you do." — some guy who sold overpriced rectangles

Seven years of silence. Not because I had nothing to say — because I was busy *doing*.

---

Heraclitus said you can't step in the same river twice. He never tried debugging the same codebase after 7 years. That river is on fire.

Anyway. I made a math game: [**afterMATH24**](https://zhang-ray.github.io/afterMATH24/)

4 cards. 60 seconds. Make 24. Sounds trivial. Try it with `1, 5, 11, 11` and get back to me.

---

Seneca wrote that we suffer more in imagination than in reality. He clearly never watched his timer hit 3 seconds with an unsolved puzzle staring back at him.

The Stoics would say: *focus on what you can control*. I say: focus on which card to tap next. Same energy.

---

Marcus Aurelius journaled every morning. I solve 24-point puzzles. Both are forms of mental discipline. One became a bestseller 2000 years later. The other... well, we'll see.

---

> "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." — da Vinci, probably looking at my single HTML file

Zero dependencies. Zero build step. Zero excuses.

The game runs on a phone, in a browser, inside Telegram, inside WeChat. Diogenes would approve — he lived in a barrel and needed nothing. I live in a single `index.html` and need nothing.

---

What's next? More games. More experiments. More shipping.

The river keeps flowing. 🌊
