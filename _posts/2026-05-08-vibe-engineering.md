The old game: write elegant code, get promoted, repeat until burnout.

The new game: describe what you want, let the machine build it, ship before lunch.

---

We used to argue about tabs vs spaces. Now we argue about which prompt gets better output. Progress.

The craftsman spent years mastering the chisel. The factory owner spent weeks designing the assembly line. Guess who retired first.

---

Three things that matter now:

1. **Speed of intent** — how fast you can describe what doesn't exist yet
2. **Tolerance for imperfection** — ship the 80%, fix the 20% live
3. **System thinking** — one person, many machines, zero employees

Everything else is nostalgia.

---

> "But what about code quality?"

The best code is the code you didn't write. The second best is the code a machine wrote while you were making coffee.

---

I used to sell my time. Now I sell systems that produce results while I sleep.

The marginal cost of my next product is approximately one cup of coffee and three hours of vibing.

If that sounds too good to be true, you're still thinking like a horse in a world of engines.
